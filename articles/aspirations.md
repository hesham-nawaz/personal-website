There are a lot of companies out there doing cool stuff but the nature of their business doesn't really leave a way for me to contribute meaningfully.

<summary>People and Culture</summary>
I want to work with people who are really good at what they do and will help me get better at what I want to do. This means talent and competence matter but so does being empathetic and supportive (not just knowing what it takes to help me grow but actually being willing to put in the work to help me get there).

Brilliant jerks aren't really my vibe. I want to be around people who I can work with without it feeling like a chore, who are friendly and easy to talk to.

Other traits that come to mind: reliable, curious, organized, ambitious, good communicators, proactive.

<summary>Business Model</summary>

It'd be nice to be able to work somewhere where my work helps people. This doesn't necessary mean curing cancer - even just contributing to a product that contributes positively to people's lives and that they enjoy using is fulfilling for me.

In other words, if someone asks me: "Why does your work matter?", I'd prefer to be able to give a clear and concise answer.

More broadly, my interests include transit/mobility, personal finance, facilitating human connection, healthcare, climate, education, trust and safety, helping people find jobs, productivity-enhancing tools, and more.
